## 1.1 Product Hunt

**URL:** https://www.producthunt.com/launch

### Submission URL / How to Submit
- Log in at producthunt.com, click "Submit" button (top right), then "New Product"
- Enter product URL to start the submission flow
- Launches can be scheduled up to 1 month in advance
- Launch preparation guide: https://www.producthunt.com/launch/preparing-for-launch

### Content Requirements & Asset Specifications

| Field | Specification |
|-------|--------------|
| **Thumbnail** | 240x240 px (square), max 3MB, GIFs allowed (animate on hover), avoid strobing/quick cuts |
| **Gallery Images** | Minimum 2, recommended 1270x760 px, GIFs supported, drag-and-drop reorderable |
| **Tagline** | Maximum **60 characters** |
| **Description** | Maximum **260 characters** |
| **Video** | YouTube links only (not private), full URL required (no shortened links) |
| **Product Name** | No emojis or descriptive text unless part of official name |
| **Makers** | Product Hunt usernames required for co-makers |
| **Shoutouts** | Limited to 3 per launch |
| **Promo Codes** | All fields required: offer, code, expiration date |

### What They DON'T Accept (Exclusions)
- Waitlisted/pre-launch products (must be currently available)
- Directories, templates, boilerplates
- Podcasts, courses, reports, events, books
- Pure services or commerce/deal sites
- Non-functional Kickstarter projects
- Hardware without prominent digital components
- Products primarily focused on immediate monetization
- Vaporware (must be ready for use)

### First Comment / Maker Comment Best Practices
- **70% of Product of the Day/Week/Month winners had a first comment by the maker**
- Recommended content for first comment:
  - Key features overview
  - Target user descriptions and use cases
  - Team story / founding story
  - Launch goals
  - Pricing or special offers
  - Request for feedback (explicitly NOT upvotes)
  - Welcome images, videos, GIFs
- Tone: Humble and helpful, avoid marketing language

### Featuring Quality Criteria
Products should excel in one or more:
- **Useful**: Practical benefit to users
- **Novel**: Innovative or unique approach
- **High Craft**: Well-designed, delightful experience
- **Creative**: Engaging or imaginative execution

### Approval Process
- All submissions receive thorough review
- Featuring decisions are final
- Contacting PH team "rarely necessary" -- they review all available info
- Same-day duplicate company launches may be rescheduled

### Self-Promotion Rules
- Do NOT ask friends or fans to upvote
- Do NOT solicit votes via external channels
- Booster/fake comments will be detected and penalized
- Engagement-based manipulation is monitored

### Step-by-Step Submission Process
1. Create Product Hunt account
2. Build hunter network (optional but recommended)
3. Click "Submit" > "New Product"
4. Enter product URL
5. Upload thumbnail (240x240 px, max 3MB)
6. Write tagline (max 60 chars)
7. Write description (max 260 chars)
8. Upload gallery images (min 2, recommended 1270x760)
9. Add YouTube video link (optional but 53% of winners include one)
10. Add makers (PH usernames)
11. Add up to 3 shoutouts
12. Add promo code (optional)
13. Schedule launch date (up to 1 month ahead)
14. Prepare first/maker comment
15. Post maker comment immediately at launch

## 1.2 Hacker News (Show HN)

**URL:** https://news.ycombinator.com/submit

### Submission URL / How to Submit
- Submit page: https://news.ycombinator.com/submit
- For Show HN: enter title starting with "Show HN:" and provide URL or text

### Title Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Show HN prefix** | Must start with exactly "Show HN:" |
| **No uppercase** | Don't use ALL CAPS for emphasis |
| **No exclamation points** | Prohibited |
| **No self-congratulatory language** | Avoid "amazing," "revolutionary," etc. |
| **No site names** | Displayed automatically by HN |
| **No gratuitous numbers** | "10 Ways To Do X" becomes "How To Do X" |
| **Use original titles** | Unless misleading or clickbait |
| **Append tags** | Add [video] or [pdf] when applicable |
| **No editorializing** | Keep titles neutral |

### Content Requirements (Show HN)
- Product/project must actually exist and be functional
- Must be runnable software or tangible items people can try
- Author must be available in the thread to discuss it
- Minimize barriers to trying it (avoid mandatory signups)
- Early-stage work is fine (doesn't need polish)
- Must be non-trivial and personally meaningful
- Created and maintained by the submitter

### What They DON'T Accept
- Blog posts, sign-up pages, newsletters, lists, reading material
- Landing pages or fundraising materials
- Minor version updates ("Foo 1.3.1 is out") -- major overhauls may qualify
- Politics, crime, sports, celebrities (unless revealing interesting phenomena)
- Content typical of TV news
- AI-generated or AI-edited comments

### Self-Promotion Rules (CRITICAL)
- Do NOT use HN primarily for promotion
- Occasional personal content OK, but not dominant behavior
- **NEVER** ask friends to upvote or comment
- No vote brigading or ring-voting
- No booster comments from friends/fans
- Username should NOT be your company/brand name
- Be transparent about your affiliation -- say "I built this"
- Drop all marketing/sales language
- Use factual, direct language only

### Ask HN Format
- Title must start with "Ask HN:"
- Text-only (no URL field)
- Used for asking the community questions

### Commenting Rules
- Be kind, no snarkiness
- Respond to strongest interpretation charitably
- No name-calling, flamebait, or internet tropes
- No shallow dismissals of others' work
- Use *asterisks* for emphasis, not UPPERCASE

### Step-by-Step Show HN Process
1. Create HN account (must be a human username, not brand)
2. Build karma by participating in discussions first
3. Go to https://news.ycombinator.com/submit
4. Title: "Show HN: [Product Name] -- [brief factual description]"
5. URL: Link to live product (not a landing page)
6. Submit
7. Monitor thread and respond to every comment personally
8. Be honest, humble, technical
9. Share the "why" and "how" of building it

- Publications: Submit via story settings > "Submit to publication" > select target > "Send for review"

### Content Requirements

| Field | Specification |
|-------|--------------|
| **Title** | No explicit character limit |
| **Subtitle** | Optional, added via "Add Subtitle" button |
| **Tags/Topics** | Maximum **5 topics per post** |
| **Word Count** | No minimum/maximum; 800-1,600 words typical for tech (4-8 min reads) |
| **Images** | Recommended 1024px+ width; 692px width for OG image; 3 display sizes available |
| **SEO fields** | Custom SEO title and description available |
| **Canonical URL** | Supported for cross-posted content |

### Formatting Options
- Bold, italic, hyperlinks
- Headers (H1, H2)
- Block quotes, pull quotes
- Bullet and numbered lists
- Code blocks (inline and block)
- Drop caps, dashes, emojis
- Superscript, footnotes
- Math expressions
- Mentions (@)
- Embeds (YouTube, Twitter, GitHub, etc.)
- Image captions

### Partner Program Requirements (2026)
- Must have published a story in the last 6 months
- Must be in an eligible country
- Must have bank account for tax filing
- **No follower requirement**
- **No minimum views requirement**
- Inactive for 6+ months = deactivated from program

### What They DON'T Accept
- Varies by publication (each has own rules)
- Platform-wide: spam, harassment, hate speech
- Some publications reject listicles, under-3-minute reads

### Self-Promotion Rules
- Varies by publication
- Generally: share valuable content, not sales pitches
- Affiliate links generally discouraged in major publications

### Link Policies
- Dofollow links by default on Medium
- Each publication may have own link policies
- Canonical URLs supported for cross-posting

### Top Tech Publications to Target
- Better Programming
- Towards Data Science
- JavaScript in Plain English
- Level Up Coding
- The Startup

### Step-by-Step Process
1. Create Medium account
2. Write story using the editor
3. Add cover image, title, subtitle
4. Add up to 5 tags
5. For personal blog: click "Publish"
6. For publication: story settings > "Submit to publication" > select > "Send for review"
7. Wait for publication editor to review

- SEO company promotions
- Spam content created solely for backlinks
- Plagiarized content
- AI-generated content (without substantial human editing)

### Formatting Requirements
- Use paragraphs, subheadings, bullet/numbered lists

## 2.3 dev.to

**URL:** https://dev.to

### Submission URL / How to Submit
- Editor: https://dev.to/new
- No approval required -- publish directly
- Editor guide: https://dev.to/p/editor_guide

### Content Requirements

| Field | Specification |
|-------|--------------|
| **Title** | Required |
| **Tags** | Maximum **4 tags**, comma-separated |
| **Cover Image** | Optimal: **1000 x 420 pixels**, URL-based |
| **Description** | Optional frontmatter field |
| **Canonical URL** | Supported for cross-posts |
| **Series** | Optional series name for grouped posts |
| **Published** | Boolean (true/false for draft status) |
| **Word Count** | No minimum/maximum |
| **Image Size Limit** | 200 megapixels per frame/page for GIFs |

### Frontmatter Format (YAML)
```yaml
---
title: Your Article Title
published: false
description: Brief description
tags: javascript, webdev, beginners, tutorial
cover_image: https://example.com/image.jpg
canonical_url: https://yoursite.com/original
series: My Series Name
---
```

### Formatting Support
- Full Markdown support
- Bold, italic, strikethrough
- Headers H1-H6
- Inline and block code (with syntax highlighting)
- Links and images with alt text and captions
- HTML comments/author notes
- Inline HTML elements
- Figure captions via HTML tags

### Liquid Tags / Embeds
- Universal URL embeds: YouTube, Twitter, GitHub, Twitch, Spotify, CodePen, Vimeo, Reddit, 20+ platforms via `{% embed URL %}`
- `{% card %}` for highlights
- `{% details %}` / `{% spoiler %}` / `{% collapsible %}` for expandable content
- `{% katex %}` for math
- `{% runkit %}` for executable code
- `{% link %}`, `{% user %}`, `{% tag %}`, `{% comment %}` for internal references

### What They DON'T Accept
- No explicit content restrictions beyond community guidelines
- Spam and purely promotional content discouraged
- Harassment and hate speech prohibited

### Self-Promotion Rules
- Self-promotional content is allowed but should provide value
- Community-first approach encouraged
- Excessive self-promotion may be flagged

### Approval Process
- **No approval needed** -- direct publishing
- Community moderators may remove policy-violating content
- Content appears in feeds immediately

### Step-by-Step Process
1. Create dev.to account
2. Go to https://dev.to/new
3. Add cover image (1000x420 optimal)
4. Write title
5. Add up to 4 tags
6. Write article in Markdown
7. Use liquid tags for embeds
8. Preview article
9. Set `published: true`
10. Click "Publish"

## 2.5 freeCodeCamp

**URL:** https://www.freecodecamp.org/news

### Submission URL / How to Submit
- Application form: Inside the style guide at https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/developer-news-style-guide/
- Draft submission: Email link to editorial@freecodecamp.org
- Platform: Drafts written on Hashnode

### Content Requirements

| Field | Specification |
|-------|--------------|
| **Minimum Word Count** | 500 words (recommended) |
| **Tutorials** | Focused pieces on defined topics |
| **Handbooks** | 5,000-15,000 words |
| **Books** | 15,000+ words |
| **Tags** | 1-5 maximum (first tag appears above tutorial) |
| **Cover Images** | Designed by freeCodeCamp team (do NOT submit your own) |
| **Image file size** | Under 1MB |
| **URL/Slug** | Short and descriptive (e.g., "machine-learning-with-pytorch-tutorial") |

### Headline Rules
- Avoid gerunds in titles ("Building X" --> "How to Build X")
- Effective structures:
  - "How to fix/build/do [X]"
  - "How to [task] with [tool]"
  - "How [something] works"
  - "What is [noun]?"
  - "The [topic] Handbook" (5K-20K words)
  - "The [topic] Book" (20K+ words)
- Include searchable keywords naturally

### What They DON'T Accept
- AI-generated art (for cover images)
- Unmodified ChatGPT-generated articles
- Plagiarized content (zero tolerance, permanent ban)
- Off-topic content (recipes, non-programming content)
- Branded/ghost-written content
- Affiliate links (unless for self-created books/courses)
- Multi-part series (prefer single comprehensive tutorials)

### Required Writing Samples
- 3 previously written articles
- If no samples: write some on dev.to or Hashnode first

### Formatting Requirements
- Simple, straightforward language
- Short sentences and paragraphs (1-2 sentences max per paragraph)
- Use H2 for main topics, H3/H4 for subsections
- Minimize exclamation marks, semicolons, ellipses
- Active voice preferred
- Use "you" instead of "we"
- Spell out non-standard acronyms
- Replace Latin abbreviations ("e.g." --> "for example")
- All code must be tested and formatted (use Prettier)
- Syntax highlighting in code blocks
- No hotlinked images -- download and upload

### Image Sources
- Pexels, Unsplash, Wikipedia (no-attribution)
- Self-created content
- Include alt text on all images
- No AI-generated art

### Self-Promotion Rules
- ONE sentence call-to-action at article end only
- Do NOT open article with product link
- No affiliate links (except self-created courses/books)
- Disclose if content is company-paid

### Cross-Posting Policy
- Prefer single-location publishing (on freeCodeCamp)
- LinkedIn cross-posting acceptable (walled garden)
- Use canonical URLs pointing to freeCodeCamp version

### Link Policies
- All links are rel="doFollow"
- Use judiciously -- valuable linking only

### Approval Process
- Application review: "only approve a small percentage"
- Rejection is not final -- can reapply
- No hard prerequisites (self-taught welcome)
- No curriculum completion required
- Non-native English speakers supported

### Review Timeline
- Application: Weeks to months (high volume)
- Article review: Editorial team reviews, edits, and may request revisions
- No fixed weekly deadlines

### Step-by-Step Process
1. Read the style guide 2-3 times: https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/developer-news-style-guide/
2. Prepare 3 writing samples (write on dev.to/Hashnode if needed)
3. Fill out application form (inside the style guide)
4. Wait for approval email with Hashnode access
5. Write draft in Hashnode editor
6. Add cover image via "Add cover" (NOT the Publish tab)
7. Add 1-5 tags
8. Click "Submit" in Hashnode settings
9. Email draft URL to editorial@freecodecamp.org
10. Respond to editorial feedback
11. Editorial team publishes when ready

## 2.6 Slashdot

**URL:** https://slashdot.org

### Submission URL / How to Submit
- Submission form: https://slashdot.org/submit
- Bookmarklet available for quick submissions from any webpage

### Content Requirements

| Field | Specification |
|-------|--------------|
| **Headline** | 6-10 words, descriptive |
| **Body Length** | ~100 words (main page stories) |
| **Links** | Multiple sources preferred |
| **Tone** | Neutral, let readers draw conclusions |
| **Format** | Text and static images preferred over video |

### What Makes a Good Submission
- **Interesting**: Major changes in laws, new technology, unique projects
- **Informative**: New information most people wouldn't know
- **Clear**: Spell out acronyms, use full names, provide context
- **Well-linked**: Intelligent hyperlinks to specific documents (not vague front pages)
- **Snappy**: Concise, around 100 words
- **Neutral**: No loaded or insulting terms (e.g., avoid "Micro$oft")
- **Properly categorized**: Select appropriate topic tags

### What They DON'T Accept
- Loaded/biased language
- Poorly sourced submissions
- Broken URLs
- Video-only submissions (text preferred)
- Overly long submissions

### Formatting Rules
- Link descriptive phrases, not generic words like "this" or "here"
- Use proper grammar and spelling
- Include context for unfamiliar topics
- Multiple links to different accounts of same event preferred

### Editorial Process
- Submissions are edited for spelling, grammar, clarity
- May be shortened to emphasize key points
- Links may be removed if not relevant
- Brackets and ellipses used for edits
- No specific acceptance rate disclosed

### Step-by-Step Process
1. Go to https://slashdot.org/submit
2. Select appropriate topic category
3. Write headline (6-10 words)
4. Write body (~100 words) with context
5. Include hyperlinks to source articles
6. Submit for editorial review
7. Editors will edit and publish if selected
