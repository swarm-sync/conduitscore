## Scoring Criteria

Each combination scored 1-5 on three axes:

- **Value Proof (VP):** Does the free user see enough to understand what the product does?
- **Upgrade Motivation (UM):** Is there a clear, felt gap that makes paying feel worthwhile?
- **Prospect Respect (PR):** Does the gate feel fair and not manipulative to a legitimate buyer?

Total score = VP + UM + PR (max 15)

Threshold for "Top Pick": 12+

## What the Scores Reveal

Three factors reliably drove high scores:

1. C4 (sample fix) appeared in all five top picks. It is the non-negotiable element. The user must touch a real fix or they cannot understand what they are buying.

2. D5 (combo gate) appeared in four of five top picks. Scan limits and depth limits serve different psychological functions. Scan limits create time-based urgency. Depth limits create immediate in-session urgency. Both together maximize conversion surface without requiring either limit to carry the whole load alone.

3. E3 (count-down prompt) outperformed E5 (before/after) and E4 (social proof) in prospect respect. Before/after requires working score projection logic. Social proof requires real, auditable numbers. Count-down framing works from the data already on the page.

The single most dangerous combination is anything in the A1-A2 + B1-B2 + C1 range: too little information to prove value means users who would have paid leave without ever understanding what they missed.
