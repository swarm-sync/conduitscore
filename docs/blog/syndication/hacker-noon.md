## The Wrong Scoreboard

You rank first for your main keyword. The curve looks healthy. Good news.

Separate question: someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity something your product solves. Three competitors get mentioned. Even a Reddit thread shows up if it helps the answer hang together. Your company doesn’t appear.

That isn’t one dashboard “lying.” **Search rankings and AI-written answers aren’t graded the same way.** One rewards pages people click after a query; the other grabs whatever looks easy to summarize and corroborate in a hurry.

For the last fifteen years, “winning SEO” mostly meant owning that Google results page someone actually clicked through. Growth leads could translate blue links into demos. **Marketing leads** could report revenue-per-keyword paths that finance understood. Models like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity do not reliably reward you for the same win—because showing up inside a synthesized answer isn’t “rank position” in the SERP sense; it’s *quotability*: can a system confidently extract who you are, what you promise, and why a third party would trust you—and then cite you without hedging?

**Search definitions split in public view.** Editors at [Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com/what-is-generative-engine-optimization-geo-444418) frame **generative engine optimization (GEO)** as positioning so AI platforms cite or mention you—not only so you occupy a clickable slot beneath the ads. GEO success metrics emphasize mentions, citations, and share-of-voice in generated answers—not rank and clicks alone.

That mismatch is exactly why this feels like betrayal when the dashboard looks stellar. **Founders** who measure pipeline from organic search assume one scoreboard got replaced silently with another.

Consider a concrete inversion: In 2023 and 2024, Reddit threads surfaced constantly in AI overview–style summaries—not because random subreddits out-SEO’d Bloomberg, but because argumentative, example-heavy human text satisfies what models excerpt under uncertainty. Translation for your roadmap: engineered corporate pages tuned for SERP CTR can still lose to messy forum paragraphs when the downstream task is *answer drafting*, not *link-click distribution*.

The uncomfortable claim—early—is this: **a site can dominate Google rankings for its category and remain structurally unreadable or untrustworthy to an AI aggregator building a stitched answer.**

## Three Breakpoints SEO Practitioners Patch After Slack Celebrates GA

### 1. Extraction friction

Ranking rewards relevance plus authority signals Google already ingests at scale. Synthetic answers disproportionately punish walls of marketing copy that refuse to declare a claim in paragraph one. Models prefer self-contained units: declarative sentences with explicit entities (“Company X sells Y for Z customers in [markets]”) versus clever slogans repeating adjectives twice.

### 2. Corroboration asymmetry

Google can lean on backlinks and behavioral metrics it measures directly. Conversational citation engines often elevate sources that collide across reputable third parties—reviews, filings, recognizable editorial outlets—until confidence crosses a probabilistic threshold. Your perfectly optimized pillar page sits alone; three strangers on G2 agreeing with each other beats one polished page insisting on greatness.

### 3. Policy and crawler reality

Blocking or constraining newer AI-oriented retrieval paths (bots, ingestion policies, fragmented `robots.txt` / `llms.txt` stories) splits your “ranking theater” from your **retrieval-ready** corpus.

Operators feel this viscerally inside **marketing leads** dashboards: CTR stable, demos flat, brand queries soft in chat interfaces.

There is upside: GEO plus classic SEO overlaps where technical clarity already paid rent—structured identifiers, factual blocks, sane HTML hierarchy, freshness on pricing and capabilities. Specialists building **AI visibility score** tooling (including scanners like **ConduitScore**) compress crawlability plus extractability checks into measurable gaps.

Treat this as segmentation, not abandonment: SERP mastery still earns clicks; GEO decides whether synthesized answers associate your brand fairly.

## Concrete bets

1. **Publish answers, not vibes.** Strip one pillar into blunt Q&A blocks. Measure excerptability.
2. **Triangulate trust off-domain.** Credible overlaps beat lonely hero pages.
3. **Instrument split metrics.** SERP CTR vs conversational prompts weekly.
