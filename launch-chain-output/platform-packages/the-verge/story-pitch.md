Subject: Story tip: The websites invisble to AI search — and the tools measuring it

Hi [Reporter name],

Story tip for your AI/tech desk:

As ChatGPT Browse and Perplexity become mainstream, a category of "AI-invisible websites" is emerging — sites that rank perfectly in Google but never appear in AI-powered answers. The cause is structural: missing AI bot permissions, outdated robots.txt rules, no llms.txt files.

A new tool, ConduitScore (conduitscore.com), scans websites and returns a 0–100 "AI visibility score." Early data from ~500 scans: median score is 42/100, and 85% of sites are missing the llms.txt file — the AI equivalent of robots.txt that most developers have never heard of.

This is the early edge of a structural shift: the infrastructure gap between Google SEO (20+ years of established practice) and AI search (2 years old, minimal tooling). The sites that build this infrastructure now will have an advantage when AI search becomes the default.

Happy to demo the scanner, share the data set, or connect you with early users.
