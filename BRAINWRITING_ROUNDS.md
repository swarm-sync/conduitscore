# DarkMirror — ConduitScore: Three Rounds of Iterative Idea Development

---

## Round 1 — What to SHOW for free that proves the product works
(6 seed ideas, fast and unfiltered)

**R1-A:** Show the full numerical score (0-100) and all 7 category subscores. The granularity proves the scanner is real and precise, not a generic tool.

**R1-B:** Show every identified issue — the full list, with titles, severity labels, and counts. A list of 15 issues is more alarming than a summary. Show the entire list.

**R1-C:** Show a plain-English explanation for each issue. Not just "missing structured data" — explain what structured data is, why it matters for AI visibility, and what the consequence of its absence is.

**R1-D:** Show the issue descriptions at full fidelity — no truncation, no blur, no "see more." The user should be able to read every word of every problem description.

**R1-E:** Show a benchmark comparison — "Your score of 43 is below the average of 61 for sites in your category." This contextualizes the score and creates social comparison urgency.

**R1-F:** Show the shareable report URL without any login requirement. The URL is permanently accessible and can be shared with a developer, a boss, an agency. This is free distribution infrastructure.

## Round 2 — What to HIDE behind paid that creates genuine upgrade motivation
(6 improved rewrites — each round 1 idea rewritten as its natural paid complement)

**R2-A (complement to R1-A):** The score history and trend. A single score proves the scanner works once. A score graph over time proves the scanner is a monitoring tool, not a one-shot audit. Historical scoring, improvement tracking, and regression alerts are paid features. The free user sees their score today. The paid user sees where they were, where they are, and whether they are improving.

**R2-B (complement to R1-B):** The code fix for each issue. The full issue list is free and alarming. The exact implementation is paid. The contrast between "I know all 15 things wrong with my site" and "I cannot fix any of them without paying" is the conversion engine. This is not frustration — it is precision tension: the user has exactly the information they need to feel the gap.

**R2-C (complement to R1-C):** The prioritized fix sequence. Knowing what is wrong in plain English is free. Knowing which fix to do first, second, and third — in order of impact on AI visibility score — is paid. This transforms the list of issues into an actionable roadmap. "Fix these 3 things first and your score will jump to 67" is a paid statement.

**R2-D (complement to R1-D):** Multi-page scan results. The free tier scans one URL. Everything the free user sees about full-fidelity issue descriptions applies to that one page. The paid tier reveals that the problem is not just one page — it is site-wide. Page-level attribution, site-wide issue aggregation, and "which pages have the most critical issues" are structural paid features.

**R2-E (complement to R1-E):** Competitor comparison. The free benchmark ("you are below average") creates urgency. The paid competitor comparison ("your top competitor scores 78 and here is why") creates competitive panic. Named competitor analysis at the domain level is an Agency-tier feature that creates immediate business justification for the budget conversation.

**R2-F (complement to R1-F):** White-label reports and client export. The shareable URL is free and drives word-of-mouth. The branded PDF export with the agency's logo, the client's name, and the prioritized fix roadmap is an Agency-tier feature. Agencies will pay $199/mo primarily to turn ConduitScore's output into a billable deliverable they can charge $500-1000/report for.

## Round 3 — How to PRESENT the locked information so it creates desire rather than frustration
(6 hybrids combining and upgrading the best of rounds 1 and 2)

**R3-A: "The Skeleton Key" pattern**
The locked code fix panel should not look like a wall — it should look like the actual fix, structurally visible but access-gated. Show the fix container with correct syntax highlighting, correct indentation, correct element structure — but with the content replaced by a repeating pattern of neutral tokens (not blurs, not asterisks — something that reads as "code is here"). The user sees the shape of the fix — a JSON-LD block, a meta tag, an htaccess rule — without seeing the content. The mental image of "there is a specific thing here that I could use right now" is more compelling than a generic "code available" message. This is the legal brief analogy: the attorney shows the client the folder with their case, not a blank piece of paper.

**R3-B: "The One Free Fix Demo" with explicit framing**
The single free code fix (from the PT model) should be the first critical-severity issue identified in the scan. It should be clearly labeled: "Free demo fix — this is exactly what paid fixes look like." The label does two things: it sets expectations (paid fixes look exactly like this one) and it frames the free fix as a product demonstration rather than a concession. The user who implements it and sees it work has now experienced the product. Every subsequent locked fix is psychologically "more of that thing that just worked."

**R3-C: "The Gap Statement" above the locked section**
Before the locked code fixes section, insert a single dynamic statement calculated from the scan result: "Fixing the 6 locked issues below would raise your score from 43 to an estimated 71." This is a score projection, not a guarantee — but it quantifies the specific value behind the gate. The user is not paying to "unlock fixes" — they are paying to go from 43 to 71. The delta is the product. The code is the mechanism. This reframes the upgrade from "pay for access" to "pay for outcome."

**R3-D: "Progressive Reveal" on hover/click**
Rather than a single binary free/locked state, locked fixes can have a micro-interaction: on hover or tap, the locked panel shows a preview line — just the first line of the code fix, visibly real and syntactically correct. "You would be adding: `<script type=\"application/ld+json\">`" — and then it stops. This is not a tease for its own sake — it confirms the fix is real, specific, and immediately implementable. It also differentiates ConduitScore from competitors who say "add structured data" without showing what that means.

**R3-E: "The Effort Estimator" per locked fix**
Each locked fix panel shows two data points alongside the lock icon: estimated implementation time and estimated score impact. Example: "2 minutes to implement — adds ~8 points." This converts the locked section from a frustration wall into a value menu. The user can see that 6 locked fixes have a combined 35-point score impact and most take under 10 minutes. They are not buying access to complexity — they are buying a 35-point score improvement that they can implement in 40 minutes. This framing is borrowed from the PT model: the patient knows how many sessions it will take before they commit.

**R3-F: "The Developer Handoff" as a conversion mechanism for non-developers**
Marketing managers who identify as non-developers have a different conversion path than technical users. For non-technical free users, the locked fix should not only show a code snippet CTA — it should show an alternative: "Send these fixes to your developer." The paid feature is a generated developer brief: a clean document with all fixes, context, and implementation instructions formatted for a developer to implement without needing to understand AI visibility. This makes the Starter/Pro plan attractive to buyers who will never touch the code themselves — they are paying for the handoff artifact, not the snippet.

## Round Summary: The Strongest Combinations

The three most powerful combinations across all three rounds:

**Combination 1 (R1-B + R2-B + R3-C):**
Show all issues free (full list, full descriptions) + lock all code fixes + add a gap statement that quantifies the score delta. The free experience creates maximum urgency; the locked section quantifies the value behind the gate; the upgrade decision is about the outcome, not about access.

**Combination 2 (R1-F + R2-F + R3-F):**
Free shareable URL for everyone + paid white-label PDF export + developer handoff brief as a paid feature. This triples the paid value for agency users without changing anything about the free experience. Agencies see three reasons to upgrade: their logo on the report, the developer brief, and the multi-page scan.

**Combination 3 (R1-D + R2-C + R3-E):**
Full issue descriptions free + prioritized fix sequence as paid + effort estimator on each locked fix. The free user understands every problem completely. The paid user knows what to fix first and how long it will take. The effort estimator visible even on locked fixes turns the locked section into a preview of the paid workflow — not a wall, but a menu.
