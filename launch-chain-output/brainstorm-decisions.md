# Launch Strategy Decisions — ConduitScore
Extracted from: brainstorm-output.md
Date: 2026-03-28

## Top Positioning Angle
"ChatGPT can't find your site — here's your score and exactly why"
Anxiety-first framing using existing vocabulary ("ChatGPT," "invisible") before introducing the "AI Visibility Score" terminology inside the product. Acquisition uses fear; retention builds the category vocabulary.

## Platform Priority Order
1. Direct agency cold outreach (email + LinkedIn, personalized free scan attached)
2. Reddit (r/SEO, r/Entrepreneur, r/webdev) — post brand scan results, not promotion
3. Twitter/X — public brand scan thread, invite "scan your site" replies
4. SEO content cluster ("AI SEO," "llms.txt," "how to get cited by ChatGPT") — 60-90 day horizon
5. Product Hunt — after social proof assembled (50+ paying customers or 500+ WAS), not day 1

## Primary Messaging Frame
"We scanned [Brand X] and found 7 reasons ChatGPT ignores it — here's the public score"
This is falsifiable (specific brand, specific count), brand-specific (creates relevance), and replicable (can be executed for any niche or audience).

## Known Failure Modes
1. [HIGH] Market education gap — target buyers don't yet feel AI search invisibility as a revenue risk; launch lands in silence without education-first content
2. [HIGH] No viral mechanic at launch — shareable report URL + embeddable badge must ship BEFORE launch amplification; without them, organic spread stalls permanently
3. [MEDIUM] Multi-channel dilution — attempting Reddit + Twitter + cold outreach + PH + SEO simultaneously with one founder produces weak signal everywhere

## Key Constraint
Founder time bandwidth is the single most fixed constraint. The product is feature-complete. All technical, channel, and trust constraints are soft or apparent. Time cannot be expanded by purchasing a credential or making a configuration change. Every channel decision is a time allocation decision.

CONFIDENCE: 38% YES
