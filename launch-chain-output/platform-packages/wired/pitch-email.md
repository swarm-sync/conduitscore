Subject: Story pitch: The emerging infrastructure gap between Google SEO and AI search

Hi [Editor/Reporter name],

The web has spent 20 years building infrastructure for Google — PageRank signals, meta tags, sitemaps. Most of it is invisible to AI-powered search.

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude use different signals: AI bot permissions in robots.txt, llms.txt files, JSON-LD structured data, author authority markers. These are new conventions, most sites don't have them, and there's no established playbook.

Data point: scanning 500 websites with ConduitScore (conduitscore.com) — a free AI visibility scanner I built — found that 85% are missing llms.txt and 40% have robot.txt configurations blocking AI crawlers.

This is an interesting moment: the web is being re-indexed by a new type of agent with different requirements, and most website owners don't know it's happening.
