## DarkMirror Analysis

### Failure Mode 1: The "So What" Problem — Nobody Cares About AI Visibility Scores Yet
**Why it happens:** The market for "AI visibility" as a defined need does not yet exist in most buyers' minds. SEO took years to become a recognized problem category. You are launching a solution to a problem that the average website owner has not yet experienced as painful. They do not check if ChatGPT cites them. They do not know what llms.txt is. The score means nothing without a felt pain point to anchor it to.
**Root cause:** Classic category creation problem — the product assumes market education has already happened. It has not. You are selling fire insurance to people who have never seen a fire.
**Falsifiable condition:** This failure is avoidable IF you can show, in the first 10 seconds of the landing page, a concrete example where a competitor's site gets cited by ChatGPT and the prospect's site does not — making the stakes visceral and personal before asking them to scan.
**Probability: HIGH**

### Failure Mode 2: Free Tier Is Friction-Heavy Enough to Kill Word of Mouth
**Why it happens:** The free tier allows 3 scans/month and shows only 1 unblurred fix. This creates a specific trap: the user runs one scan, sees a vague score number, gets one fix, and leaves with no clear understanding of what they are missing. They do not feel urgency — they feel confusion. They cannot share a compelling outcome with a colleague because the outcome is half-visible.
**Root cause:** Fix gating is optimized for conversion pressure, not for aha-moment delivery. The single visible fix may be low-stakes or obvious, making the locked fixes feel speculative rather than valuable.
**Falsifiable condition:** This failure is avoidable IF the one free fix shown is always the highest-impact fix, AND the locked fixes show enough of their title/category to create genuine FOMO rather than just a blurred box.
**Probability: HIGH**

### Failure Mode 3: The Analyzer Credibility Gap — Scores Feel Arbitrary
**Why it happens:** A 0–100 score for "AI visibility" is a number ConduitScore invented. There is no ISO standard, no Google algorithm announcement, no academic paper backing the methodology. The moment a technically sophisticated prospect asks "why is my score 43 and not 67?" and cannot get a rigorous answer, trust collapses.
**Root cause:** Proprietary scoring systems require a credibility infrastructure (methodology whitepapers, named researchers, cited studies, third-party validation) that takes time to build.
**Falsifiable condition:** This failure is avoidable IF the methodology page explains each of the 7 analyzers with specific, verifiable examples and real data.
**Probability: HIGH**

### Failure Mode 4: Agency Tier Pricing Wall With No Visible Path
**Why it happens:** The Agency tier ($149/mo) is listed as "Contact Us." In a self-serve SaaS world, "Contact Us" is a conversion graveyard for anyone who is not already sold.
**Root cause:** The "Contact Us" model assumes inbound sales motion, but the product's entire acquisition loop is self-serve.
**Falsifiable condition:** This failure is avoidable IF Agency tier converts to a self-serve checkout OR the "Contact Us" flow has a response time under 2 hours.
**Probability: MEDIUM**

### Failure Mode 5: Distribution Has No Compounding Mechanism
**Why it happens:** Launch strategy without a stated distribution engine means the product relies on launch-day spikes that decay within 72 hours. There is no embedded virality: no badge, no widget, no integration.
**Root cause:** The product is a point-in-time scanner. Unless results are actionable over time, there is no reason to return, no reason to tell others, and no organic compounding.
**Falsifiable condition:** This failure is avoidable IF at least one of: (a) shareable score card with backlink, (b) embeddable badge, or (c) integration with existing SEO tool.
**Probability: HIGH**

### Failure Mode 6: Category Matures Faster Than You Can Monetize
**Why it happens:** Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz, or a well-funded startup will add "AI visibility" as a feature within 12–18 months.
**Root cause:** The product solves a narrow, well-defined problem that is a natural extension of tools incumbents already own. The moat is thin.
**Falsifiable condition:** This failure is avoidable IF ConduitScore builds a defensible data asset (aggregated benchmark scores across industries) that incumbents cannot replicate without years of historical data.
**Probability: MEDIUM**

### Failure Mode 7: The Worst Idea Trap — Launching to "Everyone With a Website"
**Root cause:** Without a beachhead segment with a shared vocabulary, shared watering holes, and a shared pain point, the launch diffuses across too many personas to build social proof in any one community.
**Falsifiable condition:** This failure is avoidable IF the launch is sequenced: Week 1 targets one specific community, gets 50+ scans from that cohort, extracts a compelling stat, uses that stat as social proof for the next community.
**Probability: HIGH**

## Archaeologist Analysis

**Historical Analogues:**

**HubSpot Website Grader (2007/2014):** Free 0-100 score, no login. Score designed to be "slightly disappointing" to create improvement motivation. Agencies used it as client prospecting tool (B2B2C distribution HubSpot never had to build). 4M+ analyses in first years.
→ **Lesson:** The score creates the problem; the product sells the solution.

**Moz + Domain Authority (2010-2012):** Launched DA as a metric (not a product), distributed freely via MozBar extension. SEO professionals began citing DA in client reports. Moz Pro sold the improvement path.
→ **Lesson:** Name the metric. If you define the measurement standard, competitors play in your framework forever.

**GTmetrix (2010+):** Shareable report URLs at unique permalinks. Developers linked to their GTmetrix reports in forums and GitHub. Each link was inbound traffic. Report pages ranked in Google for "gtmetrix report [domain]."
→ **Lesson:** Make the report shareable and linkable. Every shared score is a backlink, referral, and demo.

**Semrush early days (2008-2012):** Published "industry reports" using proprietary data. Press coverage → brand authority → data perceived as more authoritative. Agency partnerships for client referrals.
→ **Lesson:** Turn your proprietary scan data into published benchmarks.

**Clearscope/MarketMuse (2017-2019):** Launched into algorithm anxiety moment. Agency-first distribution. Agencies reported content scores to clients, normalizing the metric. Client demand pulled individual subscriptions.
→ **Lesson:** Agencies are force multipliers.

**The ONE pattern across all successful analogues — The Score-Gap-Solution Loop:**
1. Invent and name a score that measures something real but previously unmeasured
2. Make the gap visible (benchmark comparison is essential — "your score vs. industry average")
3. Own the solution path so that improving the score requires the paid product

Secondary: Metric ownership compounds over time. GTmetrix + Semrush also show two free distribution engines: shareable report URLs + published benchmark data.

## Narrative Brief

**The one question that determines everything:** Does your target buyer already wake up worried that ChatGPT is ignoring their site, or do they first need to be convinced that this is a problem worth solving? At 38% confidence that the pain is already felt, the honest answer is: you do not yet know. That uncertainty should not paralyze the launch — it should shape the first 48 hours into a deliberate test that resolves it.

**Lead with the Crux, not the product.** The strongest positioning angle is not "here is a tool" — it is "here is what we found when we scanned [Brand X]." Lead every channel with a specific, named, scored result. This does two things simultaneously: it demonstrates the product's value before anyone signs up, and it forces the market to react. If SEO practitioners and agency owners respond with "wait, this is a real problem?", you are still in education mode. If they respond with "send me my score immediately," the pain is felt and distribution is the only variable.

**The agency channel is your highest-leverage day-one move.** Not Product Hunt. Not Reddit virality. Twenty cold emails to agencies, each containing a free scan of their own site, will tell you more about market readiness in 72 hours than any other experiment. The scan is the pitch. No deck, no demo call required. If agencies forward it to clients, you have a Trojan Horse. If they ignore it, you have a market-education problem and you pivot to content.

**Do not launch without two things built:** a shareable report URL and an embeddable badge. Every analogue — GTmetrix, Moz, HubSpot Grader — compounded through shareable outputs. Without these, the product is a closed room. With them, every scan is a distribution event.

**The fix gate is not the problem; the sequence is.** Free users should encounter the score first, understand what it measures, feel the gap between their score and a benchmark, and only then hit the fix gate. The 7-day onboarding sequence exists precisely to build that meaning before the conversion moment.

**The single most dangerous failure mode** is not competition or pricing — it is launching across Reddit, Twitter, cold outreach, SEO, and Product Hunt simultaneously with one founder. Weak signal everywhere is indistinguishable from product-market failure. Pick the agency channel. Run it hard for seven days. Then read the data.

**The one action to take today:** write the 20-agency cold outreach sequence, attach a personalized free scan of each agency's site, and send the first five. Everything else — the badge, the content cluster, the PH page — depends on what those five emails teach you about whether the market is ready to buy.
